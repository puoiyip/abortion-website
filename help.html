Many women opt to travel out-of-state to obtain an abortion if abortions are banned where they live. However, not everyone has the resources to achieve this. Telehealh is another option that allows individuals to consult with healthcare professionals using technology, such as video or phone calls. This way, people can also have medication deliverered to them.

Abortion medication can be delivered through telehealth without in-person clinical visits. Approximately 25% of abortions in the last three months of 2024 were provided through this manner. It is possible to receive telehealth care from states that have “shield” laws, which protect health care practitioners who provide care to individuals in states with abortion bans. However, there are ongoing legal disputes regarding this issue.
